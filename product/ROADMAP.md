# Implementation Roadmap

## Workstream 1 — Reference-repository migration inventory

- Inventory public routes, source fragments, generated pages, data files, calculator modules, tests, research memoranda, redirects, metadata, and deployment assumptions in `karadzhyan/hotel-wage-hour`.
- Map each artifact to retain, transform, replace, archive, or exclude.
- Establish URL and SEO parity requirements.
- Identify duplicated facts and competing source-of-truth locations.

**Exit:** approved migration ledger with owners and parity requirements.

## Workstream 2 — Canonical authority and rule registry

- Implement source, passage, rule, interpretation, effective-period, review, and dependency schemas.
- Retain immutable source artifacts and hashes.
- Define rule-authoring and attorney-approval workflows.
- Build source supersession and dependency traversal.

**Exit:** one verified legal topic flows from source capture through approved rule and revalidation event.

## Workstream 3 — Public authority-layer migration

- Migrate the issue, jurisdiction, tool, development, methodology, glossary, case, FAQ, and calendar structures.
- Preserve reviewed dates, citations, accessibility, canonical URLs, metadata, structured data, feeds, and redirects.
- Validate content parity against the migration ledger.

**Exit:** public authority plane meets agreed parity and quality gates.

## Workstream 4 — Deterministic tool extraction

- Extract calculator and audit logic into versioned rule specifications.
- Separate legal predicates, arithmetic, presentation, and scenario persistence.
- Port worked examples and boundary fixtures.
- Add approval, versioning, branch trace, limitations, and save-to-matter contracts.

**Exit:** each migrated tool is reproducible, tested, reviewable, and source linked.

## Workstream 5 — Identity, tenant, privilege, and audit foundations

- Implement SSO, tenant isolation, role/attribute policies, object permissions, privilege classification, audit events, export controls, and retention primitives.
- Complete threat model and privacy data map.

**Exit:** security architecture is independently reviewed before private enterprise facts are accepted.

## Workstream 6 — Property facts and applicability pilot

- Implement property, locality, room-count, workforce, pay, benefit, systems, provenance, freshness, and stewardship models.
- Integrate a small property cohort and one jurisdiction cluster.
- Evaluate approved rules against versioned fact snapshots.

**Exit:** one legal change generates reproducible property applicability results and disclosed fact gaps.

## Workstream 7 — Governed matters, decisions, controls, and evidence

- Implement matter lifecycle, findings, scenarios, owners, actions, approvals, decision records, control designs, evidence requests, tests, exceptions, remediation, and history.
- Link every object to its dependencies.

**Exit:** one matter moves end to end from change through approved decision and post-implementation assurance.

## Workstream 8 — Production readiness

- Manual accessibility audit
- Performance budgets and observability
- Security testing and incident response
- Backup and restoration exercise
- Content staleness and revalidation monitoring
- Release evidence and rollback procedure
- Legal, editorial, product, security, and executive approvals

**Exit:** production-readiness review is complete and residual risks are formally accepted.
