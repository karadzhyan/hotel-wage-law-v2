# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary public users are hotel operators, human-resources and payroll leaders, general managers, employment counsel, and researchers who need a reliable answer to a hospitality wage-and-hour question without losing the governing source, effective date, scope, or limitation.

The enterprise users are general counsel, employment counsel, legal operations, payroll, benefits, HRIS, and hotel operations leaders who must determine what changed, where it applies, what decision is required, who owns the response, and what evidence proves that the response operated.

## Product Purpose

Hotel Wage Law connects verified legal authority to practical hospitality action. The public experience makes the substantive wage-and-hour corpus discoverable and useful. The protected enterprise experience carries the same governed source and rule records into property applicability, matters, findings, controls, evidence, decisions, and revalidation.

Success means a user can move from a legal question to a supportable answer, and from a legal change to an accountable enterprise response, without creating competing versions of the law or hiding uncertainty.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, lucid, exacting.

The voice should feel like a first-rate hospitality employment lawyer who respects the reader's time: direct before exhaustive, calm about complexity, precise about what is known, and candid about what remains unresolved.

## Anti-references

- A generic enterprise dashboard used as the public homepage.
- A thin legal publisher or SEO directory that treats citations and review dates as decoration.
- An AI-generated landing page built from dark gradients, floating cards, repeated uppercase kickers, and unsupported claims.
- A luxury-hotel marketing site whose photography overwhelms the legal substance.
- Dashboard theater: opaque compliance scores, invented certainty, synthetic activity presented as production truth, or charts without accountable decisions behind them.
- A magazine-style editorial treatment that makes the interface look sophisticated while making the answer harder to find.

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the crown jewel.** Preserve the depth, worked examples, tools, and primary-source trail before adding presentation or enterprise polish.
2. **Answer first, prove immediately.** Lead with the direct answer, then expose scope, effective date, authority, facts, calculation, judgment, and limitations in a predictable order.
3. **One governed record, two planes.** Public authority and private enterprise context may have different permissions and density, but they must not create divergent law or duplicate sources of truth.
4. **Make uncertainty useful.** Missing facts, unsupported branches, stale sources, disputed interpretations, and pending review should produce visible next actions, not vague warnings or silent guesses.
5. **Earn executive attention.** Enterprise surfaces should organize around material decisions, accountable work, and operating proof, not around the existence of data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA is the minimum target. Every substantive workflow must work with a keyboard, visible focus, semantic structure, assistive technology, 200 percent zoom, reflow, reduced motion, and non-color status cues. Legal content must remain readable at comfortable line lengths and must not depend on tiny labels, hover-only disclosure, or low-contrast metadata.
